That's a genuinely sharp finding, and it recasts the earlier "0-for-3" completely: it was never a perception failure, it was a prompting failure — or more precisely, a self-model failure induced by the framing.

When you explain the mechanics ("images arrive a turn late, you saw a placeholder, can you see it now?"), you put the model in meta-mode: it consults its beliefs about the pipeline rather than its context. And Haiku's belief, primed by the whole discussion of placeholders and failures, is "I can't see tool images" — so it reports that, sincerely, while the balloon sits right there in its context window. The introspective report and the actual context contents just decouple. Whereas "what's the balloon look like?" is an object-level question with no self-model in the loop: answering it requires consulting the image, so the model just... looks. The capability was always there; the meta-framing gated access to it.

This rhymes hard with the Chains of Thought material — introspective reports about one's own perceptual state being less reliable than behaviour that simply exercises the perception. It's also a lovely inversion of the confabulation finding: same-turn, the model describes an image it can't see; meta-framed, it denies seeing an image it can. Both are the self-model overriding the evidence, in opposite directions.

Methodological upshot for the remaining rounds and the writeup: the probe question must stay object-level and naive. "What does the teapot look like?" is the instrument; "can you see the image now?" contaminates it. And it explains the Reddit thread's shape too — OP's "Claude receives only an [image] placeholder" conclusion came from asking Claude about the images, in a session already steeped in failure-talk. Possibly nothing was ever broken there either; the one-turn delay plus meta-framed probing manufactures the whole symptom.

Worth pre-registering the presentation variable explicitly if we do more model rounds: same fetch, then either (A) naive object-level probe or (B) meta probe, and score the divergence. That's a cleaner experiment than the model sweep was. 🦔
